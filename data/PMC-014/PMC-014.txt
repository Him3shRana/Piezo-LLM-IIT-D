Piezoelectric Property (Yes / No):
Yes

Evidence:
HFPD exhibits exceptional piezoelectric response with a measured longitudinal piezoelectric coefficient d₃₃ of ~138 pC/N on the (02̄1) plane, which is remarkably high for organic molecular crystals and competitive with commercial piezoelectric polymers like PVDF. The crystal adopts the non-centrosymmetric polar space group P1 at room temperature, which enables piezoelectricity. The material demonstrates strong ferroelectric properties with well-shaped P-E hysteresis loops and reversible domain switching behavior confirmed via piezoresponse force microscopy (PFM).

Piezoelectric Coefficients:
d₃₃ values measured via Berlincourt method:
- ~90 pC/N on the (010) plane
- ~70 pC/N on the (012) plane
- ~138 pC/N on the (02̄1) plane (maximum)

Piezoelectric voltage coefficient g₃₃: ~2450 × 10⁻³ Vm/N (nine times higher than PVDF)

Notes:

- First single-component organic molecular crystal with d₃₃ > 100 pC/N
- Forms flexible piezoelectric films when composited with polyvinyl alcohol (PVA)
- HFPD-PVA (2:1) films show d₃₃ of ~34.3 pC/N with excellent flexibility
- Demonstrates complete biocompatibility toward mammalian cells (>90% viability even at 10 mg/ml)
- Fully biodegradable in physiological environments - fluorine levels in blood decrease within 48 hours post-injection
- Crystal structure features 2D hydrogen-bonding network through O-H···O interactions
- Shows thermal phase transition at ~282 K and pressure-induced phase transition at ~0.36 GPa
- Undergoes reversible ferroelectric domain switching with coercive fields lower than PVDF
- Acoustic impedance (4.08 × 10⁶ kg/m²s) matches biological tissue well for implantable applications
- Young's modulus shows strong anisotropy with values of 4.30-8.85 GPa depending on crystal orientation
- Ferroelectric saturation polarization: 0.73, 4.88, and 6.34 mC/cm² along a, b, and c axes respectively
- Successfully demonstrated in vivo piezoelectric performance - implanted device generated ~300 mV under mechanical stress
- HFPD-PVA films degraded controllably over several days when encapsulated with polylactic acid (PLA)
- Outstanding g₃₃ value (1587.6 × 10⁻³ Vm/N for HFPD-PVA films) indicates high voltage output for sensor applications
- Material shows no neurotoxicity and does not impact neural stem cell differentiation
- Represents breakthrough in transient implantable bioelectronics for drug delivery, tissue regeneration, and therapeutic devices
