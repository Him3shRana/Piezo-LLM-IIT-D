Evidence             :
• Experimental piezoelectric measurements were performed using piezoresponse force microscopy (PFM), second harmonic generation (SHG), and a commercial d33 meter.
• The crystal exhibited a measurable longitudinal piezoelectric response of approximately 2 pC/N as a single crystal.
• Glycinium sulfamate–glycine crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric orthorhombic space group Pn (No. 7), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The crystal is composed entirely of discrete molecular species (glycinium cations, neutral glycine molecules, and sulfamate anions) held together by hydrogen bonding and ionic interactions, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
