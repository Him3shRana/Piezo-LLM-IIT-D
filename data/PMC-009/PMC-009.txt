Piezoelectric Coefficient:

Experimentally measured in the Physical Review Letters (1998) study.
The 2018 RSC Advances paper reports the following calculated piezoelectric strain coefficients (pm/V):
d₁₅ ≈ 31 pm/V
d₂₄ ≈ 18 pm/V
d₃₃ ≈ 6 pm/V
These values are in good agreement with the experimental measurements and indicate that mNA has one of the largest piezoelectric responses among organic molecular crystals.

Notes:

Single-crystal organic molecular crystal.
Non-centrosymmetric structure is responsible for its piezoelectric and electro-optic (Pockels) properties.
One of the most extensively studied organic piezoelectric crystals.
Frequently used as a benchmark material for nonlinear optics and molecular piezoelectricity.
The crystal structure deposited as MNIANL10 is the structure used in subsequent experimental and theoretical piezoelectric investigations.
Crystal System: Orthorhombic rectification rather than directly measuring piezoelectric coefficients. However, because the crystal is experimentally confirmed to be ferroelectric and belongs to the non-centrosymmetric space group Pna2₁, it is classified as a piezoelectric molecular crystal.
