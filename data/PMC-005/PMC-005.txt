Crystal ID: PMC-005

DOI: 10.7567/JJAP.53.09PD07

Molecule Name: 2-Phenylmalondialdehyde (Phenylmalondialdehyde, PhMDA)

Chemical Formula: C₉H₈O₂

Crystal Type: Organic hydrogen-bonded molecular ferroelectric crystal

Piezoelectric Property: Yes

Evidence: The crystal is a hydrogen-bonded organic ferroelectric with a non-centrosymmetric Pna2₁ (No. 33) crystal structure. The paper demonstrates terahertz-wave generation through optical rectification, a second-order nonlinear optical effect that occurs only in non-centrosymmetric crystals, supporting its piezoelectric nature. The crystal is experimentally established as a ferroelectric material.

Research Paper: Terahertz radiation by an optical rectification process in a hydrogen-bonded organic molecular ferroelectric crystal, 2-phenylmalondialdehyde, Japanese Journal of Applied Physics, 2014, DOI: 10.7567/JJAP.53.09PD07

CIF File Name: PROLON

CCDC ID: 1238105

Space Group: Pna2₁ (No. 33)

Crystal System: Orthorhombic

Piezoelectric Coefficient: Not Reported (NR)

Notes: The crystal structure was originally reported by D. Semmingsen (1977) (CSD Refcode: PROLON, CCDC Deposition 1238105). The 2014 paper investigates its terahertz emission via optical rectification rather than directly measuring piezoelectric coefficients. However, because the crystal is experimentally confirmed to be ferroelectric and belongs to the non-centrosymmetric space group Pna2₁, it is classified as a piezoelectric molecular crystal.
