Evidence             :
• Experimental piezoelectric measurements were reported by Paturle, Graafsma, Sheu, Coppens, and Becker using single crystals of 2-methyl-4-nitroaniline.
• The crystal exhibited a longitudinal piezoelectric coefficient (d33) of approximately 20 pC/N, making it one of the highest-performing small organic molecular piezoelectric crystals reported at the time.
• The crystal crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric monoclinic space group Ia (No. 9), an alternative setting of Cc (No. 9), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The crystal consists exclusively of discrete 2-methyl-4-nitroaniline molecules held together by intermolecular interactions, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
• BAJCIY01 is a refined redetermination of the same polymorph as BAJCIY, making it an appropriate crystal structure for representing the experimentally studied piezoelectric phase.
