Supramolecular Structure
------------------------
L-argininium cations form 1D hydrogen-bonded chains parallel to the
crystallographic b-axis. These chains are linked to phosphite anions via
strong O—H, N—H, and H—H interactions, creating a 3D hydrogen-bonded
supramolecular network. The major intermolecular contacts are:
  - O—H interactions    : 47.7% (dominant, red colour on Hirshfeld surface)
  - H—H interactions    : 41.5%
  - N—H interactions    : 5.0%
  - C—H interactions    : 4.0%
  - N—O interactions    : 1.0%
  - O—C interactions    : 0.5%

Remarks
-------
The CIF corresponds to the L-Argininium Phosphite (LAPI) structure 
(CCDC 1026960, 2015). This is an organic-inorganic hybrid material 
combining a natural amino acid (L-arginine) with the phosphite ion.

Key findings from the 2019 Applied Physics A study:

(1) LAPI demonstrates simultaneous piezoelectric (d22 = 12 pC/N) and
    nonlinear optical (SHG = 20 mV ≈ 84% KDP) activity, making it a
    multifunctional material for integrated photonic devices.

(2) The 3D hydrogen-bonded supramolecular network (analyzed by Hirshfeld
    surface) is the key structural feature enabling both piezo and NLO
    properties through anisotropic polarization.

(3) Mechanical strength is excellent: hardness up to 9 kg/mm², robust
    laser resistance (6.3 J/cm² at single shot; 0.63 GW/cm²), and ability
    to withstand 12 shock pulses (Mach 2.2) without fracture.

(4) High crystalline perfection (FWHM = 46 arcsec for (200) Bragg peak)
    confirms low defect concentration—essential for NLO applications.

(5) The 1D chain structure along b-axis, combined with 3D network linking,
    produces strong anisotropic properties. The optical bandgap of ~5.1 eV
    ensures low absorption in the visible-NIR range, suitable for photonic
    systems.

(6) Compared to conventional NLO materials (KDP, ADP, urea, lithium
    niobate), LAPI offers biocompatibility, ease of synthesis from
    naturally available amino acids, and a unique combination of
    piezoelectricity + pyroelectricity + SHG activity in one material.

This work establishes LAPI as a promising candidate for:
  - Nonlinear optical (NLO) devices
  - Piezoelectric energy harvesting and sensing
  - Image processing and ultrasonic transducers
  - Integrated photonic systems combining multiple functionalities
