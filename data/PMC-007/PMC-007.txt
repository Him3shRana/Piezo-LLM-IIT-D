Crystal ID: PMC-007

CCDC ID: LALNIN01

Molecule Name: L-Alanine

Chemical Formula: C₃H₇NO₂

Crystal Type: Molecular Crystal (Amino Acid Crystal)

Piezoelectric Property: YES 

Evidence:

The paper experimentally demonstrates that L-alanine nanocrystals generate piezoelectric current under applied mechanical pressure.
The authors detected mechanical pressures as low as 3 Pa using L-alanine nanocrystals.
The paper explicitly states that L-alanine possesses a non-centrosymmetric crystal structure (Space Group P2₁2₁2₁), which allows piezoelectricity.
It also gives the piezoelectric tensor corresponding to the crystal symmetry and explains that polarization originates from deformation of hydrogen bonds under mechanical stress.

Research Paper:
Hanna Bishara, Alina Nagel, Maya Levanon, Shlomo Berger,
Amino acids nanocrystals for piezoelectric detection of ultra-low mechanical pressure,
Materials Science & Engineering C 108 (2020) 110468,
DOI: 10.1016/j.msec.2019.110468

CIF File Name: PMC-007-L-Alanine.cif

Space Group: P2₁2₁2₁ (No. 19)

Crystal System: Orthorhombic rectification rather than directly measuring piezoelectric coefficients. However, because the crystal is experimentally confirmed to be ferroelectric and belongs to the non-centrosymmetric space group Pna2₁, it is classified as a piezoelectric molecular crystal.

Piezoelectric Coefficient:
The paper does not experimentally measure numerical piezoelectric coefficients (dij).

Notes:

Single-crystal molecular crystal.
Naturally non-centrosymmetric.
Piezoelectricity arises from deformation of the hydrogen-bond network between zwitterionic L-alanine molecules.
Nanocrystals were grown inside anodic alumina nanopores with preferred crystallographic orientation.
Used as an ultra-sensitive piezoelectric pressure sensor.
Detects pressures down to 3 Pa.
The crystal structure used in the paper matches the standard L-alanine crystal reported in the literature (Acta Crystallographica, 1966), corresponding to LALNIN01 in the CSD.
