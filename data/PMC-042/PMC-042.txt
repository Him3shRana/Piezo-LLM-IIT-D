Crystal Type: Organic molecular crystal (dipeptide)

Piezoelectric Property: Yes

Evidence: The crystal belongs to the non-centrosymmetric space group P2₁2₁2₁ (orthorhombic), which is compatible with piezoelectricity. The 2025 paper by Morozova et al. in Applied Materials Today reports the first piezoresponse force microscopy (PFM) study of Phe-Leu micro- and nanocrystals. Both vertical and lateral piezoelectric signals were detected. The effective piezoelectric coefficients were determined as d_eff ≈ 71 pm V⁻¹ (vertical displacement) and d_eff ≈ −73 pm V⁻¹ (lateral displacement). These values are comparable to or exceed those reported for diphenylalanine (Phe-Phe) nanocrystals (d_eff = 30 pm V⁻¹ lateral, d₃₃ = 20 pm V⁻¹).

Notes:

Pure organic dipeptide molecular crystal (hydrophobic dipeptide).
Non-centrosymmetric crystal structure (P2₁2₁2₁), compatible with piezoelectricity.
Original crystal structure reported by Görbitz (2001) in the context of nanotube formation by hydrophobic dipeptides (CCDC 163339).
The crystal cell is elongated along the c-axis, consistent with the needle-like crystal habit observed by AFM.
Two independent molecules (Z' = 2) in the asymmetric unit, with Z = 8.
The 2025 paper by Morozova et al. reports the first measurement of piezoelectric properties of Phe-Leu crystals using PFM.
Crystals were grown on silicon substrates by methanol vapor saturation of spin-coated amorphous dipeptide films.
Phe-Leu crystals: elongated structures up to 10 μm long, 0.3–0.5 μm wide, up to 30 nm high.
The polarization vector of the piezoelectric domains is oriented at an angle to the surface, as both vertical and lateral PFM signals are present.
The piezoelectric coefficients (71 and 73 pm V⁻¹) significantly exceed those of polyhydroxybutyrate fibrillar structures (d₃₃ ≤ 2.5 pm V⁻¹).
Companion dipeptide L-leucyl-L-phenylalanine (Leu-Phe) forms monoclinic crystals with d_eff ≈ 87 pm V⁻¹ (vertical) and ≈ −19 pm V⁻¹ (lateral).
Biocompatible material with potential applications in biomedicine, green energy, and piezoelectric nanodevices.
