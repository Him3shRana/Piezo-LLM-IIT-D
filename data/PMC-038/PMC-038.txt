Remarks
-------
The CIF corresponds to the classical L-Tyrosine crystal structure
(refcode LTYROS10, 1972) with face-to-face dimer building blocks as
the basic unit. In the 2019 ACS Nano study, L-Tyr was identified as
the least soluble among three aromatic amino acids (L-Phe, L-Tyr,
L-DOPA) due to strong hydroxyl-mediated dimer formation, creating a
three-dimensional hydrogen-bonded network. This unique packing
accounts for the highest thermal stability and mechanical rigidity.
L-Tyr crystals are mechanically and optically functional: a film-based
nanogenerator produced high piezoelectric outputs; incorporation of
rigid L-Tyr needles in soft PDMS polymer enabled mechano-responsive
bending and surface patterning; and simple dye incorporation allowed
optical waveguiding—demonstrating potential for energy harvesting,
soft robotics, photonic devices, and stretchable electronics.
