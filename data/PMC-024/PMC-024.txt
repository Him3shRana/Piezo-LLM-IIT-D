Piezoelectric Property : Yes

Evidence             :
• Quantitative piezoelectric measurements were reported for both single crystals and highly oriented thin films of imidazolium perchlorate.
• The crystal exhibits robust ferroelectric polarization switching and room-temperature piezoelectric response.
• Imidazolium perchlorate crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric trigonal space group R3m (No. 160), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The crystal is an organic molecular salt composed of discrete imidazolium cations and perchlorate anions and is recognized as an organic molecular ferroelectric.
