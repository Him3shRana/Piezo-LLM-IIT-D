Evidence             :
• Experimental piezoelectricity was demonstrated in the Matter (2025) study using piezoresponse force microscopy (PFM) on single crystals.
• Density Functional Theory (DFT) predicted a very large shear piezoelectric coefficient (d15) of approximately 99.19 pC/N for 2-chloro-3-hydroxypyridine.
• Experimental measurements showed an effective shear piezoelectric response of approximately 54–74 pC/N and a longitudinal piezoelectric response (d33) of approximately 5–10 pC/N.
• The crystal crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric orthorhombic space group Fdd2 (No. 43), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The crystal consists entirely of discrete 2-chloro-3-hydroxypyridine molecules linked by hydrogen bonding and halogen bonding, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
