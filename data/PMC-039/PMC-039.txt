Crystal Packing Description
---------------------------
Molecules aggregate into alternating layers of L and D isomers (homochiral layers).
Within each layer, molecules are interconnected through head-to-tail sequences
generated by cell translation and 2₁ screw axes. Adjacent layers are interconnected
by head-to-tail sequences generated by glide planes. This parallel packing of 
alternating L and D molecules results in net dipole polarization along the crystal
axes—in contrast to L-alanine, where antiparallel packing cancels dipoles.

Remarks
-------
The CIF corresponds to the racemic DL-Alanine structure (CCDC 164668, 2001)
with Pna21 symmetry. Crucially, DL-alanine differs from L-alanine in crystal
packing: the parallel arrangement of alternating L and D layers (vs. antiparallel
in L-alanine) produces a net crystal dipole moment along the cell axes, enabling
piezoelectricity. The 2019 Physical Review Letters study demonstrated that:

(1) DFT predictions for DL-alanine agree with experimental PFM measurements
    on single crystals (9.1 pm/V ≈ predicted 10.3 pm/V).
(2) Open-circuit voltage of 0.8 V is twice that of L-alanine or phage viruses,
    and approaches one-half of a dry-cell battery (1.5 V).
(3) The absence of required molecular chirality for piezoelectric behavior is a
    paradigm-shifting finding, applicable to achiral materials and racemic mixtures.

This work establishes DL-alanine crystals as viable candidates for biocompatible
piezoelectric transducers in energy harvesting, force sensing, and actuator
applications where the lower cost and simpler synthesis of racemic materials
over chiral analogs offer significant practical advantages.
