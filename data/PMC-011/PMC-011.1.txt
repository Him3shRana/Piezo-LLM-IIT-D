Evidence             :
• Experimental crystal structure determined by synchrotron single-crystal X-ray diffraction and deposited in the Cambridge Structural Database (CCDC 909441; CSD REFCODE: KOWYEA02).
• The crystal crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric orthorhombic space group Pn (No. 7), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The γ-polymorph exhibits ferroelectricity arising from cooperative proton transfer (prototropy), as first reported by Horiuchi et al. (Nature Communications, 2012).
• The piezoelectric properties were experimentally measured by Horiuchi et al. (Journal of Materials Chemistry C, 2018) using the Berlincourt method (direct piezoelectric effect).
• The experimentally measured longitudinal piezoelectric coefficient is d33 = 7.5 ± 0.4 pC/N.
• The crystal consists of discrete neutral 2-methyl-1H-benzimidazole molecules linked through intermolecular hydrogen bonds, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
