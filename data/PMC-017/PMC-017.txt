Molecule Name: trans-4-Hydroxy-L-proline

IUPAC Name: (2S,4R)-4-Hydroxypyrrolidine-2-carboxylic acid

Chemical Formula: C₅H₉NO₃

Crystal Type: Organic molecular crystal (amino acid)

Piezoelectric Property: Yes

Evidence: The crystal belongs to the non-centrosymmetric space group P2₁2₁2₁, which permits shear piezoelectricity. The 2024 Physical Review Letters paper by Hari et al. demonstrates that molded polycrystalline assemblies of trans-4-hydroxy-L-proline exhibit measurable longitudinal piezoelectricity (d₃₃ up to 2.2 pC/N) despite the orthorhombic symmetry only formally permitting shear response in single crystals. This longitudinal response arises from the random orientation of crystallites in the polycrystalline assembly. The assemblies also produced a maximum peak-to-peak open circuit voltage of 700 mV under manual tapping.

Research Paper: Molded, Solid-State Biomolecular Assemblies with Programmable Electromechanical Properties

Notes:

Pure organic amino acid molecular crystal (no co-former or salt).
Non-centrosymmetric crystal structure (P2₁2₁2₁), compatible with shear piezoelectricity.
Original crystal structure deposited in 1952 (CCDC 1177934; CSD refcode HOPROL01); HOPROL12 is the reference used in the 2024 paper (Table I).
The 2024 paper presents a novel molded polycrystalline fabrication technique and reports longitudinal piezoelectric response (d₃₃) for the first time in substrate-free trans-4-hydroxy-L-proline assemblies.
Young's modulus of polycrystalline assembly: ~19 GPa (compression testing).
Piezoelectric voltage constant g₃₃ = 26 mV m/N (for disc of 3.3 cm diameter).
Operational temperature range: 298–568 K (decomposition onset ~568 K from DSC).
Crystals fuse together during crystallization into a cohesive polycrystalline bulk, making them good candidates for device integration.
Known biological role: conotoxin component, collagen constituent.
