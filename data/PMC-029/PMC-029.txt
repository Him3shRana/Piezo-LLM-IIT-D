Evidence             :
• Experimental crystal structure determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction and deposited in the Cambridge Structural Database (CCDC 796166; CSD REFCODE: TAPZIT01).
• The crystal crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric monoclinic space group Pn (No. 7), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The α-polymorph exhibits ferroelectricity originating from cooperative proton transfer (prototropy), as reported by Horiuchi et al. (Advanced Materials, 2011).
• The piezoelectric properties were experimentally measured by Horiuchi et al. (Journal of Materials Chemistry C, 2018) using the Berlincourt method (direct piezoelectric effect) and laser interferometry (converse piezoelectric effect).
• The experimentally measured longitudinal piezoelectric coefficient is d33 = 7.3 ± 0.8 pC/N.
• The crystal consists of discrete neutral HPLN molecules held together by intermolecular hydrogen bonding, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
