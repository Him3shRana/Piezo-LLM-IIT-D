Evidence
--------
Complete piezoelectric tensor experimentally determined via
X-ray three-beam diffraction at the Brazilian National
Synchrotron Light Source (XRD1 beamline). Renninger scan
technique used with polarimeter-like diffractometer. Silver
electrodes painted on crystal faces; DC electric field
applied via variable-voltage low-current power supply.
Maximum electric field: 5 × 10⁴ V/m (75 V for a-cut,
100 V for c-cut).

Piezoelectric Properties (experimental)
----------------------------------------
Method: X-ray Three-Beam Diffraction (Renninger scan technique)

Complete piezoelectric tensor (monoclinic point group 2):

        [100]     [010]     [001]     [011]     [101]     [110]
Ex (    0         0         0         4(2)      0         230(4)   ) × 10⁻¹¹ C/N
Ey (    510(5)    220(3)    62(8)     0         14(1)     0        ) × 10⁻¹¹ C/N
Ez (    0         0         0         110(2)    0         220(4)   ) × 10⁻¹¹ C/N

Individual coefficients:
d14 = (4.0 ± 0.2) × 10⁻¹¹ C/N
d16 = (2.3 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁹ C/N
d21 = (6.5 ± 0.8) × 10⁻⁹ C/N  (confirmed by rocking-curve measurement)
d22 = (2.20 ± 0.03) × 10⁻⁹ C/N
d23 = (0.62 ± 0.08) × 10⁻⁹ C/N
d25 = (0.14 ± 0.01) × 10⁻⁹ C/N
d34 = (1.1 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁹ C/N
d36 = (2.2 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁹ C/N

Measurement directions:
  Electric field in [100] (X): d14 and d16 measured
  Electric field in [010] (Y): d21, d22, d23, d25 measured (previous work)
  Electric field in [001] (Z): d34 and d36 measured

Primary reflections used:
  (0,0,10) for E applied along X
  (5,0,0) for E applied along Z
  (800) for rocking-curve d21 confirmation

Intermolecular Interactions
---------------------------
Hydrogen bonding (chloride–guanidyl group interactions)

Isostructural Analogues
-----------------------
L-Arginine Hydrobromide Monohydrate (L-AHBR.H2O)
  Note: L-AHBR.H2O is softer than L-AHCL.H2O due to weaker
  hydrogen-bond strength between bromide and guanidyl group
  compared to chloride and guanidyl group. Piezoelectric
  coefficients differ significantly between the two.

Non-Linear Optical Properties
-----------------------------
L-AHCL belongs to the group of semi-organic crystals with
non-linear optical properties. Extensively studied for
frequency conversion applications (Monaco et al., 1987).
Synthesized originally as one of ten L-arginine salts with
NLO properties for Nd:YAG laser frequency conversion.

Crystal Growth
--------------
Slow evaporation of an aqueous solution of commercial powder
at controlled temperature. Single crystals of good optical
quality (rocking-curve FWHM ~30 arcsec by synchrotron
radiation).

Remarks
-------
The CIF corresponds to the original 1970 structure
determination by Dow & Jensen (refcode ARGHCL10).
The complete piezoelectric tensor was successfully measured
using X-ray three-beam diffraction, completing the
characterization started in 2003. The L-arginine molecule
is present as a dipolar ion (zwitterion) in the crystal.
This is the first complete piezoelectric tensor measurement
for L-AHCL.H2O using three-beam diffraction techniques.
