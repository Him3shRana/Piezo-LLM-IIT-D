Evidence             :
• Experimental crystal structure determined by single-crystal X-ray diffraction and deposited in the Cambridge Structural Database (CCDC 276855).
• L-Methionine crystallizes in the non-centrosymmetric monoclinic space group P21 (No. 4), which is crystallographically compatible with piezoelectricity.
• The crystal consists of discrete neutral L-methionine molecules connected through hydrogen-bonding interactions, confirming its molecular crystal nature.
• Experimental studies have demonstrated the piezoelectric behavior of L-methionine crystals through piezoelectric nanogenerator devices, producing measurable electrical output under mechanical deformation.
• Recent studies have also investigated oriented L-methionine thin films for enhanced piezoelectric performance.
